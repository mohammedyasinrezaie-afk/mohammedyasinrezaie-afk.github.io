Low-Cost Electric Wheelchair Prototype

Year: 2023 (Year 11 Systems Engineering)

Overview

Designed and prototyped a scaled electric wheelchair system aimed at reducing the high cost of mobility devices. The prototype demonstrated differential steering, torque optimisation, and load testing under constrained power conditions.

Problem

Commercial electric wheelchairs in Australia range between $2,000–$18,000. The goal was to explore a lower-cost electrical mobility system capable of carrying scaled loads and maintaining control reliability.

Design Approach

  • Arduino microcontroller
  • Motor shield (H-bridge control)
  • 4 modified servo motors
  • Joystick analog input control
  • Laser-cut chassis
  • 9V battery supply

Iteration

Initial 5V DC motors lacked sufficient torque and drew excessive current. They were replaced with modified servo motors (~1.8 kg·cm stall torque), improving load handling and efficiency.

Testing & Results

The prototype successfully carried a 1 kg load (900 mL water bottle) while maintaining directional control. Differential steering allowed smooth turning under load.

Future Improvements

  • Replace servos with geared DC motors
  • Improve wheel traction
  • Add Bluetooth module
  • Upgrade to dual 18650 battery system
  • Design full protective enclosure
